Title: VALS framework by Arnold Mitchell

----

Text: <p><strong><a href="https://blog.crobox.com/article/psychographic-research" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow">Psychographic segmentation</a></strong>&nbsp;is the process of creating clusters of customers who share similar characteristics and then grouping them together. These clusters will form subsets (or segments) of customers based on their:&nbsp;</p><ul><li><p>Social class</p></li><li><p>Lifestyle</p></li><li><p>Interests</p></li><li><p>Values</p></li><li><p>Personality</p></li><li><p>Social status</p></li><li><p>Religion</p></li><li><p>Opinions</p></li><li><p>Attitudes</p></li><li><p>Hobbies</p></li></ul><p>This article will talk you through the ones in bold.&nbsp;</p><p>Back in 1980,&nbsp;<strong><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Psychographic_segmentation" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow">Arnold Mitchell developed VALS</a></strong>&nbsp;(values, attitudes, lifestyles) which became a technique for market research - this provided the springboard for psychographic segmentation.&nbsp;</p>
